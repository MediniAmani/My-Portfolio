# Rachel How — Fave App Redesign: sectioning

Source: [Fave App Redesign](https://www.rachelhow.com/design/fave-app-redesign)

This note extracts how the case study is **sectioned** (nav spine, chapter hierarchy, and repeating content patterns). It is about structure, not a full content dump.

---

## 1. Page chrome (above the narrative)

| Layer | What it is |
| --- | --- |
| Eyebrow / series label | `Fave App Revamp` |
| Hero headline | One outcome-led line (who + scale): redesigning for 4M users |
| Meta row | `Timeline` · `Platform` · `My role` |
| Sticky section nav | Jump links for the main chapters (see below) |

Meta is short and factual. Role depth lives later in the body, not only in the meta strip.

---

## 2. Sticky nav spine (primary sectioning)

These are the **top-level chapters** the page is designed around:

1. **Introduction**
2. **Problem**
3. **Goal** (page body title: **Goals**)
4. **Impact**
5. **Our Users**
6. **Process**
7. **Final Designs**
8. **Learnings** (page body title: **What we learned**)
9. **Future**

Pattern: a short, fixed spine (setup → problem → goals → proof → people → method → product → reflection → next). Product UI walkthrough is one chapter (`Final Designs`), not the whole page.

---

## 3. Full outline (nav + nested body sections)

### Hero / meta

- Timeline, Platform, My role

### Introduction

- Project / company context
- Scope of the revamp
- **My Role** (collaborators named; sits under the intro block even though it is not a nav item)

### Problem

- Short framing line
- Four numbered problem cards:
  - **Problem #1 — No Relevancy**
  - **Problem #2 — No Defined Journey**
  - **Problem #3 — Brand & Product Perception**
  - **Problem #4 — Inconsistent Experience**
- Each card uses the same internal beat: **PROBLEM → EXAMPLE → IMPACT**
- Bridge: **Defining the problem** (problem statement beat before goals)

### Goals (`Goal` in nav)

Split into two labeled groups:

- **Business goals**
  - Strengthen core product value proposition
  - Operational efficiency / scalable components
- **User goals**
  - Increase content relevancy
  - Delightful, consistent consumer experience

### Impact

- Phase-release context
- Metrics block (values omitted for confidentiality on this page)

### Our Users

- Research framing (behavioral data + interviews)
- Jobs-to-be-done framing
- Three archetypes, each with a short profile + **JOBS-TO-BE-DONE** vignette:
  - The Habitual Creature
  - The Modern Saver
  - The Routine Breaker

### Process

Nested method chapters (roughly chronological):

1. **Design Sprints**
2. **User Flows** (before vs after; tap-event percentages)
3. **Sketches**
4. **Early designs** (with product-area subheads)
   - Home Tab
   - Explore Tab
   - Collections
   - Iteration volume callout (200+ iterations)
5. **Usability Testing & Customer Interviews**

### Final Designs

Product walkthrough as many small, singular-purpose subsections:

1. Looks new. Feels like home. (before/after framing)
2. Introduction of the Lucid Design System
3. Your home is personalised to you
4. Designed for discovery: The Explore Tab
5. Discover by Categories
6. Discover eCards across different categories
7. Improved Brand page
8. Revamped Deal Page
9. eCards with clearer messaging
10. Humanized copy and delightful illustrations
11. My Savings
12. My Faves

Each subsection is usually: one idea → short rationale → one or more visuals → optional metric.

### Learnings (`What we learned`)

Three reflection points:

1. Adapt to changing consumer behaviour
2. Products don’t exist in a vacuum
3. Take it one phase at a time

### Future

Three forward items:

1. Bug fixes
2. Post-launch optimization
3. Continue to design better experiences

### Closing / footer of the study

- Thanks + team credit
- “More to explore” cross-links (not part of the sticky spine)

## 4. Recurring micro-patterns (inside sections)

| Pattern | Where it appears | Shape |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Labeled problem beat | Problem #1–#4 | `PROBLEM` / `EXAMPLE` / `IMPACT` |
| Goal buckets | Goals | `BUSINESS GOALS` vs `USER GOALS` |
| JTBD vignette | Our Users | Archetype name → short description → `JOBS-TO-BE-DONE` scenario |
| Process stages | Process | Method label → short copy → artifact image(s) |
| Product chapter | Final Designs | Outcome headline → why it matters → screen(s) → optional metric |
| Lesson / next list | Learnings, Future | Short titled bullets, not long essays |

---

## 5. Structural takeaways

1. **Nine sticky chapters** keep orientation; depth lives in nested H2/H3s, especially under Process and Final Designs.
2. **Problem is diagnosed before solution**: numbered problems with Example/Impact, then Goals, then Impact metrics.
3. **Users sit before process and final UI**, with JTBD as the bridge into design work.
4. **Process is methods + discarded work**, not the polished product dump.
5. **Final Designs is a product tour** of many small one-job screens, not one mega collage.
6. **Learnings and Future close the loop**; they are first-class nav items, not an afterthought paragraph.
7. **Nav labels can be shorter than body titles** (`Goal` vs `Goals`, `Learnings` vs `What we learned`).

---

## 6. Spine as a reusable template

```text
Hero + meta (timeline / platform / role)
→ Introduction (+ role detail)
→ Problem (numbered, PROBLEM / EXAMPLE / IMPACT)
→ Goals (business + user)
→ Impact (metrics)
→ Our Users (archetypes + JTBD)
→ Process (sprints → flows → sketches → early UI → testing)
→ Final Designs (screen-by-screen product chapters)
→ Learnings
→ Future
```
